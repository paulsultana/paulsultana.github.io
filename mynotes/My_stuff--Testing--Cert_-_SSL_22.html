1. Check certificate with

openssl

(most detailed)

openssl s_client -connect auma.de:443 -servername auma.de

What this does:

  • Connects to the server over TLS
  • Shows the full certificate chain
  • Uses SNI (-servername) → important for proxied domains

👉 What to look for in the output:

✅ Issuer (who issued the cert)

issuer=...

  • Let’s Encrypt → origin or WGP LE cert
  • Something else → custom cert or WGP-managed

✅ Subject (domain name)

subject=CN = auma.de

✅ Validity dates

notBefore=...

notAfter=...

🔎 Cleaner version (filter important info):

openssl s_client -connect auma.de:443 -servername auma.de 2>/dev/null | openssl x509 -noout -issuer -subject -dates

🌐 2. Quick check with

curl

curl -Iv https://auma.de

What you’ll see:

  • TLS handshake info
  • Certificate subject
  • Expiration

🧠 3. Check if you're hitting WGP vs origin

This is important in your case.

👉 Check headers:

curl -I https://auma.de

Look for:

  • WEDOS headers (proxy)
  • or origin server headers

👉 Force direct origin test (if you know origin IP):

curl -Iv --resolve auma.de:443:ORIGIN_IP https://auma.de

This bypasses WGP and connects directly to origin.
